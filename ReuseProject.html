User awareness of social media algorithms


Mentor: Daniel Klug

Project Abstract
Short-form video apps like TikTok have become highly popular due to their widespread accessibility for online social interaction. In particular, TikTok’s success is attributed to its effective, yet mysterious, algorithm that curates individual video feeds based on users’ interactions with the app. As such, TikTok has received significant attention in popular media, the news, and academic research about its potential adverse impact on users. Our study focuses on motivations for the nonuse of TikTok, particularly through the lens of user awareness of TikTok’s unique algorithm. We conducted qualitative interviews with 23 TikTok nonusers to investigate how their algorithm awareness and perception of TikTok have informed their choice of nonuse. We found various reasons for nonuse, including a lack of gratification, toxic culture, missing social relations, and especially wasted time. The perceived tradeoffs for nonuse can also be interpreted as a conflict between a user’s desired control and sense of agency over the TikTok app – a personalized experience mediated by the algorithm.
Michelle Dong, Daniel Klug, Audrey Ming Wai Yip. 2023. The Ball's In Your Court. Algorithm Awareness and the Nonuse of TikTok. REU 2023 Poster Session. Carnegie Mellon University. August 3rd, 2023 (PDF).
